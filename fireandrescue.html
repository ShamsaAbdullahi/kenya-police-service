FIRE AND RESCUE

Fire and Rescue Srevices need to collect information to assess risk in their areas as well as protect the health and safety of their workers. There are four key responsibilities,they must ensure that they make provision for including: extinguishing fires in their area, protecting life and property in the event of fires in their area, rescuing and protecting people in the event of a road traffic collision and rescuing and protecting people in the event of other emergencies.

The priorities of Fire and Rescue are as follows:

  • identify and assess the full range of foreseeable fire and rescue related risks their areas face, make provision for prevention and protection activities and respond to incidents appropriately
  • work in partnership with their communities and a wide range of partners locally and nationally to deliver their service
  • be accountable to communities for the service they provide
